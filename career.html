Project Overview

For my first major project, I created professional career materials, including my resume and cover letter, for a software engineering role. This project helped me practice adapting my technical experience for a professional audience, especially recruiters and hiring managers. Instead of simply listing tasks, I focused on framing my work around impact, measurable results, and transferable skills. I revised my bullet points to use a clear action-plus-impact structure and emphasized experiences that connect to software engineering, technical problem-solving, and collaboration. This project illustrates my growth as a technical communicator because it shows how I can translate technical work into concise, persuasive, and audience-aware language.

What This Shows

This project highlights my ability to communicate technical experience clearly, tailor information to a specific audience, and revise language to make my accomplishments more concrete and compelling.

Revision and Process

One key revision I made in this project was restructuring my resume bullet points to follow a consistent action-plus-impact format. Earlier versions listed responsibilities, but after feedback, I focused on quantifying results and emphasizing impact. This made my experience clearer, more concise, and more compelling for recruiters.
