Project Overview

For my third major project, my team created a training guide for leaders of small Georgia Tech Registered Student Organizations. The goal was to make important information about budgeting, room reservations, event planning, and recruitment easier for student leaders to find and use. Because the audience is busy and may be unfamiliar with Georgia Tech’s administrative processes, I focused on making the guide practical, skimmable, and easy to navigate. The guide uses headings, links, visuals, and TL;DR sections to help readers quickly locate the information they need. This project illustrates my ability to design technical communication that is not just informative, but genuinely usable for a specific audience.

What This Shows

This project shows my understanding of audience-centered design. I learned how structure, spacing, headings, and short summaries can make a document more usable for readers who need quick, practical guidance.
